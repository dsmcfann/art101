Challenge

In this lab, we needed to use psuedocode to describe both an everyday task, as well as the steps to playing a game.

Results

Task One: Pseudocode an Everyday Task. For this, we decided to talk about how to get to the BayTree Bookstore from the Porter/Rachel Carson Bus Stop.

  1. Be present at the Rachel Carson/Porter Bus Stop.
  2. Watch towards the left for oncoming cars and busses. Wait until you see either a loop bus or a Santa Cruz Metro City bus.
    1. If the bus is a Santa Cruz Metro bus, take out your student ID.
  3. When the bus arrives at the bus stop, get into a line with all other students waiting to board the bus.
  4. Board the bus with the other students.
    1. If on a Santa Cruz Metro Bus, show bus driver your student ID.
  5. Find a seat on the bus.
    1. Walk to an open seat.
    2. Sit down in the seat.
    3. If no other seats are available...
      1. Find an open space to stand.
      2. Grab onto a handle in the bus, and hold on.
  6. Remain on the bus for 5 stops.
  7. After the bus leaves from the College 9/10 stop, reach up your hand and pull the stop cord.
    1. Wait for the bus to stop at Hagar Dr.
    2. Get off bus when bus stops.
  8. Facing the bus stop, turn to your right and walk across the street.
    1. Continue to walk forward.
    2. When you walk to the middle of the square, turn to the left.
    3. Look at the sign that says Baytree Bookstore.
  9. Walk into the open door of the Baytree Bookstore.
